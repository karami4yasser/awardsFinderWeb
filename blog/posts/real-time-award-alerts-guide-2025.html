Real‑time Award Alerts: Complete 2025 Guide

Set alerts that catch real seats. Learn the filters that matter, when to monitor, and how to act fast.

1) Pick a target route and window

Alerts work best when they’re specific. Choose origin, destination, date range (at least 7–21 days), plus desired cabin and max stops.

2) Add programs you can actually use

Select partner programs you can transfer to (Amex, Chase, Citi, CapOne, Bilt). This focuses alerts on redemptions you can book quickly.

3) Set filters that move the needle

  • Cabin: Economy for families or Business/First for premium redemptions.
  • Stops: 0–1 for most trips, 2 if you’re flexible.
  • Aircraft: Optional, but helpful when you want a specific product (ex: A350, 787).

4) Understand release patterns

Airlines release seats at different times: far‑out (330 days), rolling weekly, last‑minute, and post‑schedule‑change. Keep your alert window broad enough to catch waves.

5) Act fast and verify

When an alert hits, verify on the partner site and then transfer. Avoid transfers until you can hold/book to prevent stranded points.

Pro tips

  • Use multiple alliances to widen options (Star, Oneworld, SkyTeam).
  • Watch transfer bonuses and align partner choice to maximize value.
  • Set backup alerts (alternate dates/nearby airports).
